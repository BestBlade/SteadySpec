# Research Paper Recipe

This recipe shows how the SteadySpec anti-drift method transfers outside software. It is intentionally small: use it as a mapping example, not a full academic workflow.

## Mapping

- Intent record: research question, thesis statement, outline, or approved proposal.
- Working medium: literature notes, outline, draft sections, advisor comments, and revision log.
- Unit of work: one argument, section, experiment summary, literature review cluster, or revision pass.
- Observable check: citation audit, advisor review, source comparison, argument consistency pass, data/table verification, or journal requirement check.
- Output-vs-intent check: compare each section against the original question and contribution claim.
- Human-owned decision: scope tradeoff, ethical risk, target venue, interpretation of ambiguous evidence, or advisor preference.
- Finalized record: submitted manuscript package, revision memo, or defended thesis archive.

## Flow

1. Write the intent record before drafting: question, contribution, scope, excluded claims, and validation checks. See METHOD.md section 1.
2. Recover context before confidence: separate confirmed literature, uncertain memory, unsupported assumptions, and missing sources. See METHOD.md section 2.
3. Pressure-test the thesis: ask what evidence would break it and what alternative framing would survive. See METHOD.md section 3.
4. Draft one unit at a time and attach an observable check, such as citation support or data consistency. See METHOD.md section 4.
5. Review each section against the original question, not just against style or readability. See METHOD.md section 5.
6. Sync durable records: update outline, source notes, claims list, and revision log when the draft changes what is true. See METHOD.md section 6.
7. Finalize without truth drift: record unresolved limitations, human-owned scope decisions, and evidence gaps instead of hiding them in polished prose. See METHOD.md section 7.
8. Roll up repeated friction: if several sections keep failing for the same reason, revisit the research question or contribution claim. See METHOD.md section 8.

## Transfer Note

The paper is not treated as code. The shape is the same: keep intent visible, keep checks close to production, separate evidence from judgment, and make the final record preserve what actually happened.
